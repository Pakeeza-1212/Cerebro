Understanding stress


Introduction

Stress is a natural physiological response to a perceived threat or pressure, triggering the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. When faced with a stressful situation, the body's "fight or flight" response is activated, preparing it to either confront the challenge or flee from it. Chronic stress, however, can have negative effects on physical and mental health, including anxiety, depression, insomnia, digestive problems, and a weakened immune system. Effective stress management techniques include exercise, meditation, deep breathing, time management, and seeking social support, which can help mitigate the adverse effects of stress and promote overall well-being.

Here are the causes of stress:


1. Major Life Changes: Significant events like moving, job changes, or relationship issues can trigger stress.

2. Work-Related Stressors: Long hours, heavy workload, conflicts with colleagues, and job insecurity can contribute to stress.

3. Financial and Health Concerns: Financial difficulties, health problems, and caring for a loved one can also cause significant stress.
